ROSE: Retrieval-Oriented Segmentation Enhancement

CVPR

Song Tang, Guangquan Jie, Henghui Ding, Yu-Gang Jiang

AI Summary

Existing MLLM segmentation models struggle with novel or emerging entities due to their inability to incorporate up-to-date knowledge. To address this, the paper introduces the Novel Emerging Segmentation Task (NEST) and proposes ROSE: Retrieval-Oriented Segmentation Enhancement, a plug-and-play framework. ROSE integrates Internet Retrieval-Augmented Generation with textual and visual prompt enhancers, and an intelligent WebSense module to efficiently retrieve and leverage real-time information. Experimental results show ROSE significantly boosts performance on the NEST benchmark, outperforming a strong Gemini-2.0 Flash retrieval baseline by 19.2 gIoU.

From $P(y|x)$ to $P(y)$: Investigating Reinforcement Learning in Pre-train Space

arXiv

Yuqiao Tan, Minzheng Wang, Bo Liu, Zichen Liu, Tian Liang, Shizhu He, Jun Zhao, Kang Liu

AI Summary

Current reinforcement learning for LLMs is limited by the base model's pre-existing output distribution, and traditional pre-training inadequately optimizes the marginal distribution P(y) for targeted reasoning enhancement. The authors propose PreRL, which applies reward-driven online updates directly to P(y), and identify Negative Sample Reinforcement (NSR) within PreRL as a critical mechanism for pruning incorrect reasoning spaces. Leveraging this, Dual Space RL (DSRL) initializes models with NSR-PreRL to expand reasoning before transitioning to standard RL. DSRL consistently outperforms strong baselines, validating PreRL's viability as a surrogate and demonstrating effective policy steering towards refined correct reasoning subspaces.

Don't Let the Video Speak: Audio-Contrastive Preference Optimization for Audio-Visual Language Models

arXiv

Ami Baid, Zihui Xue, Kristen Grauman

AI Summary

Audio-Visual Language Models (AVLMs) are bottlenecked by video-driven audio hallucination, where models exploit visual shortcuts to invent sounds rather than relying on true auditory evidence. To combat this visual dominance, the paper proposes Audio-Contrastive Preference Optimization (ACPO), a dual-axis preference learning framework. ACPO employs an output-contrastive objective to penalize visually-derived audio descriptions and an input-contrastive objective that swaps audio tracks to ensure generation is sensitive to the true auditory signal. Extensive experiments demonstrate ACPO establishes faithful audio grounding and mitigates hallucination without compromising broader multimodal capabilities.

Lagrangian correspondences for moduli spaces of Higgs bundles and holomorphic connections

arXiv

Panagiotis Dimakis, Duong Dinh, Shengjing Xu

AI Summary

The paper constructs Lagrangian correspondences between moduli spaces of rank-$n$ Higgs bundles and holomorphic connections, and Hilbert schemes of points on $T^*C$ and twisted cotangent bundles, respectively. These correspondences are built upon Higgs bundles and holomorphic connections that are transversal to line subbundles, naturally inducing divisors and auxiliary parameters. The authors provide evidence that these constructions generically realize the Dolbeault geometric Langlands correspondence, expecting their quantization to realize the de Rham geometric Langlands correspondence.

HiVLA: A Visual-Grounded-Centric Hierarchical Embodied Manipulation System

arXiv

Tianshuo Yang, Guanyu Chen, Yutian Chen, Zhixuan Liang, Yitian Liu, Zanxin Chen, Chunpu Xu, Haotian Liang, Jiangmiao Pang, Yao Mu, Ping Luo

AI Summary

End-to-end Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models for robotic manipulation struggle with a trade-off where fine-tuning compromises the reasoning capabilities of their underlying Vision-Language Models (VLMs). HiVLA proposes a visual-grounded-centric hierarchical framework that decouples high-level VLM planning, which generates subtask instructions and target bounding boxes, from low-level motor control. A novel cascaded cross-attention Diffusion Transformer (DiT) action expert translates these plans into robust physical actions. This decoupled architecture preserves VLM reasoning and significantly outperforms end-to-end baselines, excelling in long-horizon skill composition and fine-grained manipulation of small objects in cluttered scenes.

ID and Graph View Contrastive Learning with Multi-View Attention Fusion for Sequential Recommendation

arXiv

Xiaofan Zhou, Kyumin Lee

AI Summary

The core problem is the limited exploration of multi-view contrastive learning between ID-based and graph-based representations for sequential recommendation, particularly when only interaction data is available. The proposed method, MVCrec, integrates complementary signals from sequential (ID-based) and graph-based views using three contrastive objectives: within each view and critically, across views. A multi-view attention fusion module then combines these representations. Key results show MVCrec consistently outperforms 11 state-of-the-art baselines on five datasets, achieving up to 14.44% improvement in NDCG@10 and 9.22% in HitRatio@10.

Relative Langlands duality and Koszul duality

arXiv

Alexander Braverman, Michael Finkelberg, Roman Travkin

AI Summary

Under the assumption of the local conjecture of Ben-Zvi, Sakellaridis, and Venkatesh for a pair of $S$-dual hyperspherical varieties, this work establishes a categorical equivalence. Specifically, if one variety is a polarized cotangent bundle, its $\mathbb{Z}/2$-graded $B$-equivariant category of quantized modules is shown to be equivalent to the $\mathbb{Z}/2$-graded unipotent $B^\vee$-monodromic category for the $S$-dual variety. This demonstrates a concrete realization of Relative Langlands duality in a geometric and categorical context.

Towards Unconstrained Human-Object Interaction

arXiv

Francesco Tonini, Alessandro Conti, Lorenzo Vaquero, Cigdem Beyan, Elisa Ricci

AI Summary

The core problem in Human-Object Interaction (HOI) detection is the reliance on predefined interaction vocabularies, which limits real-world applicability. This work introduces the Unconstrained HOI (U-HOI) task, eliminating the need for a fixed interaction list at both training and inference. Key results demonstrate the limitations of current HOI detectors and highlight the significant value of MLLMs for unconstrained interaction recognition.

A Comparative Study of Dynamic Programming and Reinforcement Learning in Finite Horizon Dynamic Pricing

arXiv

Lev Razumovskiy, Nikolay Karenin

AI Summary

This paper addresses the challenge of effective pricing in finite-horizon dynamic pricing problems by systematically comparing Fitted Dynamic Programming (DP) and Reinforcement Learning (RL). The proposed method applies both approaches to environments of increasing structural complexity, ranging from single to multi-typology settings with heterogeneous demand and inter-temporal constraints, extending DP to richer, multi-dimensional contexts. arXiv

Gitesh Malik

AI Summary

Reinforcement learning for power grid operation faces significant challenges due to strict safety requirements, brittleness, and poor generalization to unseen topologies. AI Summary

arXiv

AI Summary
