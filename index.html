MoRight: Motion Control Done Right

arXiv

Shaowei Liu, Xuanchi Ren, Tianchang Shen, Huan Ling, Saurabh Gupta, Shenlong Wang, Sanja Fidler, Jun Gao

AI Summary

Existing motion-controlled video generation methods fail to disentangle object motion from camera viewpoints and model causal relationships between user actions and scene dynamics. MoRight addresses this by specifying object motion in a canonical view and transferring it via temporal cross-view attention for disentangled control, while also decomposing motion into active and passive components to learn and predict causal consequences. This framework enables both forward and inverse reasoning for motion control under arbitrary camera viewpoints. Experiments demonstrate state-of-the-art performance in generation quality, motion controllability, and interaction awareness on three benchmarks.

TAMEn: Tactile-Aware Manipulation Engine for Closed-Loop Data Collection in Contact-Rich Tasks

arXiv

Longyan Wu, Jieji Ren, Chenghang Jiang, Junxi Zhou, Shijia Peng, Ran Huang, Guoying Gu, Li Chen, Hongyang Li

AI Summary

TAMEn addresses the limitations of handheld data collection for contact-rich bimanual manipulation, specifically hardware adaptability, low replayability, and the difficulty of capturing interactive tactile recovery data. The engine features a cross-morphology wearable interface and a dual-modal pipeline (MoCap and VR) that integrates large-scale tactile pretraining with human-in-the-loop recovery demonstrations. Experimental results show that TAMEn significantly improves demonstration replayability and boosts visuo-tactile policy success rates from 34% to 75% across diverse tasks.
