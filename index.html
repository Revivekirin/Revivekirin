UniGRPO: Unified Policy Optimization for Reasoning-Driven Visual Generation

arXiv

Jie Liu, Zilyu Ye, Linxiao Yuan, Shenhan Zhu, Yu Gao, Jie Wu, Kunchang Li, Xionghui Wang, Xiaonan Nie, Weilin Huang, Wanli Ouyang

AI Summary

The core problem addressed is the lack of a unified policy optimization framework for interleaved reasoning-driven visual generation, where models first expand a user prompt through reasoning and then synthesize an image. The proposed method, UniGRPO, formulates this multimodal process as a Markov Decision Process and introduces a unified reinforcement learning framework using GRPO to jointly optimize text and image generation policies. It integrates standard GRPO for reasoning and a modified FlowGRPO for visual synthesis, with key modifications for scalability and robustness. Key results demonstrate that UniGRPO significantly enhances image generation quality through reasoning, establishing a robust and scalable baseline for future fully interleaved models.

arXiv

Zhen Li, Zian Meng, Shuwei Shi, Wenshuo Peng, Yuwei Wu, Bo Zheng, Chuanhao Li, Kaipeng Zhang

AI Summary

ACL

AI Summary

End-to-End Efficient RL for Linear Bellman Complete MDPs with Deterministic Transitions

ACL

Zakaria Mhammedi, Alexander Rakhlin, Nneka Okolo

AI Summary

Previous computationally efficient RL algorithms for linear Bellman complete MDPs are constrained by small action spaces or strong oracle assumptions. This paper proposes a new computationally efficient algorithm for linear Bellman complete MDPs with deterministic transitions. For finite action spaces, the method is end-to-end efficient; for large/infinite action spaces, it requires only a standard argmax oracle. The algorithm learns an $\varepsilon$-optimal policy with polynomial sample and computational complexity in the horizon, feature dimension, and $1/\varepsilon$.

arXiv

Yiqi Zhang, Huiqiang Jiang, Xufang Luo, Zhihe Yang, Chengruidong Zhang, Yifei Shen, Dongsheng Li, Yuqing Yang, Lili Qiu, Yang You

AI Summary

RL training for LLMs is bottlenecked by the rollout phase, specifically due to slow autoregressive generation of long trajectories and synchronization overhead. SortedRL proposes an online length-aware scheduling strategy that reorders rollout samples by length, prioritizing shorter ones for early updates to enable large, flexible batches and a near on-policy micro-curriculum. This method, supported by off-policy control and dedicated infrastructure, reduces RL training bubble ratios by over 50% and achieves 3.9% to 18.4% superior performance over baselines.

Deformation quantization for systems with second-class constraints in deformed fermionic phase space

arXiv

Bing-Sheng Lin, Tai-Hua Heng

AI Summary

The core problem addressed is the quantization of systems with second-class constraints, requiring the use of Dirac brackets instead of Poisson brackets. The proposed method employs deformation quantization by defining a star product where the linear $\hbar$ term is proportional to the Dirac bracket. Applying this to an oscillator system in a deformed fermionic phase space, the key results include the evaluation of energy levels and Wigner functions, and an analysis of the entanglement entropy induced by the phase space deformation.

Rectify, Don't Regret: Avoiding Pitfalls of Differentiable Simulation in Trajectory Prediction

arXiv

Harsh Yadav, Christian Bohn, Tobias Meisen

AI Summary

Open-loop trajectory models struggle with compounding errors, while fully differentiable closed-loop simulators suffer from shortcut learning where future ground truth non-causally leaks into past predictions. To address this, the paper introduces a detached receding horizon rollout, which explicitly severs the computation graph between simulation steps. This forces the model to learn genuine reactive recovery behaviors from drifted states. The approach significantly reduces target collisions by up to 33.24% compared to fully differentiable methods and 27.74% against open-loop baselines, while also improving multi-modal prediction diversity and lane alignment.
