Efficient Reasoning on the Edge

arXiv

Yelysei Bondarenko, Thomas Hehn, Rob Hesselink, Romain Lepert, Fabio Valerio Massoli, Evgeny Mironov, Leyla Mirvakhabova, Tribhuvanesh Orekondy, Spyridon Stasis, Andrey Kuzmin, Anna Kuzina, Markus Nagel, Ankita Nayak, Corrado Rainone, Ork de Rooij, Paul N Whatmough, Arash Behboodi, Babak Ehteshami Bejnordi

AI Summary

Large language model (LLM) chain-of-thought reasoning is impractical for edge deployment due to verbose traces, large context requirements, and high costs. The authors propose enabling reasoning in small LLMs via LoRA adapters with supervised fine-tuning, enhanced by budget forcing via reinforcement learning to reduce response length and parallel test-time scaling for accuracy. This method, combined with dynamic adapter-switching and KV-cache sharing, achieves efficient, accurate reasoning on Qwen2.5-7B under strict resource constraints, making LLM reasoning practical for mobile devices.

MessyKitchens: Contact-rich object-level 3D scene reconstruction

arXiv

Junaid Ahmed Ansari, Ran Ding, Fabio Pizzati, Ivan Laptev

AI Summary

Object-level 3D scene reconstruction, particularly ensuring physical plausibility with realistic object contacts in cluttered environments, remains a significant challenge. This work introduces MessyKitchens, a new dataset providing high-fidelity ground truth for cluttered, contact-rich scenes, and proposes a Multi-Object Decoder (MOD) extending SAM 3D for joint scene reconstruction. MessyKitchens significantly improves previous datasets in registration accuracy and inter-object penetration for benchmarking, and MOD demonstrates consistent and significant state-of-the-art improvements in multi-object reconstruction across various datasets.

ManiTwin: Scaling Data-Generation-Ready Digital Object Dataset to 100K

arXiv

Kaixuan Wang, Tianxing Chen, Jiawei Liu, Honghao Su, Shaolong Zhu, Minxuan Wang, Zixuan Li, Yue Chen, Huan-ang Gao, Yusen Qin, Jiawei Wang, Qixuan Zhang, Lan Xu, Jingyi Yu, Yao Mu, Ping Luo

AI Summary

Robotic manipulation learning in simulation is hindered by a lack of scalable and diverse data-generation-ready digital assets. ManiTwin proposes an automated pipeline that transforms a single image into a simulation-ready, semantically annotated 3D object twin. This pipeline was used to construct ManiTwin-100K, a dataset of 100K high-quality 3D assets equipped with physical properties, language descriptions, and manipulation proposals, demonstrating efficient asset synthesis and diverse data generation for various downstream tasks.

MolmoB0T: Large-Scale Simulation Enables Zero-Shot Manipulation

arXiv

Abhay Deshpande, Maya Guru, Rose Hendrix, Snehal Jauhri, Ainaz Eftekhar, Rohun Tripathi, Max Argus, Jordi Salvador, Haoquan Fang, Matthew Wallingford, Wilbert Pumacay, Yejin Kim, Quinn Pfeifer, Ying-Chun Lee, Piper Wolters, Omar Rayyan, Mingtong Zhang, Jiafei Duan, Karen Farley, Winson Han, Eli Vanderbilt, Dieter Fox, Ali Farhadi, Georgia Chalvatzaki, Dhruv Shah, Ranjay Krishna

AI Summary

This paper challenges the assumption that effective sim-to-real transfer requires real-world data or fine-tuning for robot manipulation. The proposed method utilizes MolmoBot-Engine, an open-source pipeline for large-scale, procedurally generated simulated data (MolmoBot-Data, 1.8M trajectories), to train policies like MolmoBot, a multi-frame vision-language model. Key results show MolmoBot achieves 79.2% zero-shot real-world success on tabletop pick-and-place, outperforming existing methods like $π_{0.5}$ (39.2%), demonstrating that extensive simulation alone can enable robust, generalized manipulation policies across diverse tasks and platforms.

DreamPlan: Efficient Reinforcement Fine-Tuning of Vision-Language Planners via Video World Models

arXiv

Emily Yue-Ting Jia, Weiduo Yuan, Tianheng Shi, Vitor Guizilini, Jiageng Mao, Yue Wang

AI Summary

Vision-Language Models (VLMs) struggle with physical grounding for robotic manipulation, causing errors, and direct real-world fine-tuning is prohibitively expensive. DreamPlan proposes a method where initial sub-optimal VLM data trains an action-conditioned video world model, which then serves as a virtual environment for fine-tuning the VLM planner using Odds Ratio Policy Optimization (ORPO). This approach efficiently injects physical knowledge into the VLM via virtual rollouts, significantly improving manipulation success rates without extensive real-world data.

BrickSim: A Physics-Based Simulator for Manipulating Interlocking Brick Assemblies

arXiv

Haowei Wen, Ruixuan Liu, Weiyi Piao, Siyu Li, Changliu Liu

AI Summary

Existing rigid-body simulators inadequately capture snap-fit mechanics in interlocking brick assemblies, posing a challenge for robotic manipulation. BrickSim is introduced as the first real-time physics-based simulator for such assemblies, featuring a compact force-based mechanics model for snap-fit connections solved with a structured convex quadratic program and a hybrid simulation architecture. It achieves 100% accuracy in static stability prediction on real-world assemblies with an average solve time of 5ms, and faithfully reproduces real-world structural collapse dynamics. This high-fidelity simulation capability makes it a valuable tool for robotic construction and dexterous manipulation research.

Dynamic Meta-Layer Aggregation for Byzantine-Robust Federated Learning

arXiv

Reek Das, Biplab Kanti Sen

AI Summary

Federated Learning faces vulnerabilities from diverse Byzantine adversaries, specifically complex untargeted attacks like multi-label flipping that bypass conventional defenses. To address this, FedAOT introduces a meta-learning-inspired aggregation framework that dynamically weights client updates based on reliability without relying on fixed thresholds or attack-specific assumptions. Experimental results demonstrate that FedAOT significantly improves model accuracy and resilience against various poisoning attacks while maintaining computational efficiency and cross-dataset generalizability.

Internalizing Agency from Reflective Experience

ICML

Rui Ge, Yichao Fu, Yuyang Qian, Junda Su, Yiming Zhao, Peng Zhao, Hao Zhang

AI Summary

Current post-training methods for LLM agents often over-rely on final success signals, neglecting rich environmental feedback and failing to develop the "recovery agency" necessary for complex, long-horizon tasks. The proposed LEAFE framework addresses this by summarizing feedback into actionable experiences, backtracking to explore revised actions, and distilling these reflective corrections into the model via supervised fine-tuning. Evaluated on interactive coding and agentic tasks, LEAFE consistently outperforms outcome-driven baselines like GRPO, achieving significant gains in Pass@1 and up to a 14% improvement in Pass@k.

Stochastic Resetting Accelerates Policy Convergence in Reinforcement Learning

arXiv

Jello Zhou, Vudtiwat Ngampruetikorn, David J. Schwab

AI Summary

Stochastic resetting—intermittently returning an agent to a fixed reference state—is proposed to accelerate policy convergence in reinforcement learning, particularly for tasks with sparse rewards and difficult exploration. In both tabular and deep reinforcement learning environments, resetting enhances value propagation by truncating long, uninformative trajectories without altering the optimal policy. The method speeds up learning even in scenarios where it provides no search-time advantage for non-learning agents, establishing it as a robust mechanism for optimization.

Learning to Present: Inverse Specification Rewards for Agentic Slide Generation

arXiv

Karthik Ragunath Ananda Kumar, Subrahmanyam Arunachalam

AI Summary

This work addresses the difficulty of automated slide generation by introducing an RL environment where LLM agents use tools and an "inverse specification reward" that assesses quality based on a model's ability to reconstruct the original prompt from generated slides. By fine-tuning Qwen2.5-Coder-7B via GRPO, the method achieves 91.2% of Claude Opus 4.6’s performance, representing a 33.1% improvement over the base model. The results demonstrate that instruction adherence and tool-use compliance are more critical for agentic task success than raw parameter count.

Real-Time Decoding of Movement Onset and Offset for Brain-Controlled Rehabilitation Exoskeleton

arXiv

Kanishka Mitra, Satyam Kumar, Frigyes Samuel Racz, Deland Liu, Ashish D. Deshpande, José del R. Millán

AI Summary

Traditional robotic rehabilitation often lacks direct brain-contingent control, limiting its ability to engage neural circuits for neuroplasticity. This study implements real-time, dual-state EEG motor imagery to initiate and terminate upper-limb exoskeleton movement, introducing a class-agnostic fixation-based recentering method to mitigate signal drift and classification bias. Results demonstrate reliable start-stop control with mean hit rates exceeding 61% and significant improvements in classification separability (AUC gains up to 56%), facilitating more precise, intention-driven rehabilitation.
