Efficient Reasoning on the Edge

arXiv

Yelysei Bondarenko, Thomas Hehn, Rob Hesselink, Romain Lepert, Fabio Valerio Massoli, Evgeny Mironov, Leyla Mirvakhabova, Tribhuvanesh Orekondy, Spyridon Stasis, Andrey Kuzmin, Anna Kuzina, Markus Nagel, Ankita Nayak, Corrado Rainone, Ork de Rooij, Paul N Whatmough, Arash Behboodi, Babak Ehteshami Bejnordi

AI Summary

Large Language Model (LLM) chain-of-thought reasoning is impractical for edge deployment due to verbosity, high resource demands, and inefficient distillation into smaller models. This work proposes a lightweight approach using LoRA adapters with supervised fine-tuning for small LLMs, enhanced by reinforcement learning for budget forcing to reduce response length, and parallel test-time scaling for accuracy. Further optimizations include dynamic adapter-switching and KV-cache sharing to improve on-device inference speed and resource efficiency. Experiments demonstrate efficient, accurate reasoning on Qwen2.5-7B under strict resource constraints, making LLM reasoning practical for mobile devices.

MessyKitchens: Contact-rich object-level 3D scene reconstruction

arXiv

Junaid Ahmed Ansari, Ran Ding, Fabio Pizzati, Ivan Laptev

AI Summary

Addressing the difficulty of achieving physically-plausible monocular 3D scene reconstruction in cluttered environments, this paper introduces **MessyKitchens**, a dataset providing high-fidelity ground truth for object shapes and precise contacts. The authors also propose the **Multi-Object Decoder (MOD)**, an extension of SAM 3D designed for joint object-level reconstruction. Experimental results demonstrate that MOD significantly improves registration accuracy and reduces inter-object penetration, achieving state-of-the-art performance across three distinct benchmarks.

ManiTwin: Scaling Data-Generation-Ready Digital Object Dataset to 100K

arXiv

Kaixuan Wang, Tianxing Chen, Jiawei Liu, Honghao Su, Shaolong Zhu, Minxuan Wang, Zixuan Li, Yue Chen, Huan-ang Gao, Yusen Qin, Jiawei Wang, Qixuan Zhang, Lan Xu, Jingyi Yu, Yao Mu, Ping Luo

AI Summary

Robotic manipulation learning is currently constrained by a lack of diverse, simulation-ready digital assets at scale. To address this, ManiTwin provides an automated pipeline that transforms single images into semantically annotated 3D assets complete with physical properties and verified manipulation proposals. AI Summary

DreamPlan: Efficient Reinforcement Fine-Tuning of Vision-Language Planners via Video World Models

arXiv

Emily Yue-Ting Jia, Weiduo Yuan, Tianheng Shi, Vitor Guizilini, Jiageng Mao, Yue Wang

AI Summary

Vision-Language Model (VLM) planners lack the physical grounding necessary for complex robotic tasks, yet fine-tuning them through real-world reinforcement learning is prohibitively expensive and unsafe. DreamPlan addresses this by training an action-conditioned video world model from exploratory data and then fine-tuning the VLM planner within this virtual environment using Odds Ratio Policy Optimization (ORPO). This approach effectively bridges semantic reasoning with physical grounding, significantly increasing manipulation success rates without requiring large-scale real-world interaction.

BrickSim: A Physics-Based Simulator for Manipulating Interlocking Brick Assemblies

arXiv

Haowei Wen, Ruixuan Liu, Weiyi Piao, Siyu Li, Changliu Liu

AI Summary

Existing rigid-body simulators fail to accurately capture snap-fit mechanics in interlocking brick assemblies, hindering realistic robotic manipulation. BrickSim, a real-time physics simulator, addresses this with a compact force-based snap-fit mechanics model solved by a structured convex quadratic program, leveraging a hybrid simulation architecture. It achieves 100% accuracy in static stability prediction on 150 real-world assemblies and faithfully reproduces dynamic structural collapse, enabling high-fidelity simulation of assembly, disassembly, and robotic construction.

Dynamic Meta-Layer Aggregation for Byzantine-Robust Federated Learning

arXiv

Reek Das, Biplab Kanti Sen

AI Summary

Federated Learning is highly susceptible to diverse Byzantine attacks, including multi-label flipping and untargeted poisoning, which compromise global model performance and are inadequately addressed by existing, specific defenses. We propose FedAOT, a novel meta-learning-inspired adaptive aggregation framework that dynamically weights client updates based on their reliability to suppress adversarial influence without restrictive assumptions. FedAOT significantly improves model accuracy and resilience across various untargeted attack types and datasets, demonstrating robust, computationally efficient, and scalable performance.

Internalizing Agency from Reflective Experience

ICML

Rui Ge, Yichao Fu, Yuyang Qian, Junda Su, Yiming Zhao, Peng Zhao, Hao Zhang

AI Summary

Conventional outcome-driven post-training for autonomous agents neglects rich environmental feedback, limiting a model's ability to learn the recovery behaviors necessary for complex, long-horizon tasks. To address this, the LEAFE framework summarizes environment feedback into actionable experiences, backtracks to trial alternative actions, and distills these successful corrections into the policy via supervised fine-tuning. Across interactive coding and agentic tasks, LEAFE consistently outperforms RL baselines like GRPO, achieving up to a 14% improvement in Pass@128 and superior Pass@1 performance.

Stochastic Resetting Accelerates Policy Convergence in Reinforcement Learning

arXiv

Jello Zhou, Vudtiwat Ngampruetikorn, David J. Schwab

AI Summary

Reinforcement learning often suffers from slow policy convergence due to inefficient exploration and long, uninformative trajectories in sparse-reward environments. The authors propose stochastic resetting, a mechanism that intermittently returns the agent to a fixed reference state to truncate unproductive paths and enhance value propagation. Results across tabular and deep reinforcement learning tasks demonstrate that resetting accelerates convergence while preserving the optimal policy, offering a superior alternative to temporal discounting for optimizing learning speed.

Learning to Present: Inverse Specification Rewards for Agentic Slide Generation

arXiv

Karthik Ragunath Ananda Kumar, Subrahmanyam Arunachalam

AI Summary

This work addresses the difficulty of automated presentation generation by introducing an RL-based framework where agents learn to research and design HTML slides using tools. The proposed method utilizes "inverse specification rewards," where an LLM evaluates quality by attempting to reconstruct the original prompt from the generated slides, combined with GRPO fine-tuning of a Qwen2.5-Coder-7B model. Results demonstrate that the 7B model achieves 91.2% of Claude Opus's performance and a 33.1% improvement over its base version, highlighting that tool-use compliance is more critical than parameter count for agentic tasks.

Real-Time Decoding of Movement Onset and Offset for Brain-Controlled Rehabilitation Exoskeleton

arXiv

Kanishka Mitra, Satyam Kumar, Frigyes Samuel Racz, Deland Liu, Ashish D. Deshpande, José del R. Millán

AI Summary

To address the limited neural engagement of conventional robot-assisted therapy, this study implements a dual-state motor imagery EEG system for real-time initiation and termination of an upper-limb exoskeleton. The authors introduce a class-agnostic fixation-based recentering method to mitigate signal drift and class-driven bias without requiring command-class sampling. Experimental results demonstrate reliable online control with hit rates exceeding 61% for both onset and offset, while the proposed recentering method significantly improved threshold-free separability (AUC gains up to 56%) compared to standard techniques.
