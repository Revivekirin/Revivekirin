Generation Models Know Space: Unleashing Implicit 3D Priors for Scene Understanding

arXiv

Xianjin Wu, Dingkang Liang, Tianrui Feng, Kui Xia, Yumeng Zhang, Xiaofan Li, Xiao Tan, Xiang Bai

AI Summary

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) suffer from "spatial blindness," struggling with fine-grained geometric reasoning and physical dynamics due to reliance on limited explicit 3D data. This work proposes VEGA-3D, a framework that leverages the implicit spatial priors within large-scale video generation models, repurposing a pre-trained video diffusion model as a Latent World Simulator. It extracts spatiotemporal features from intermediate noise levels and integrates them with MLLM semantic representations via an adaptive gated fusion mechanism. VEGA-3D significantly outperforms state-of-the-art baselines across 3D scene understanding, spatial reasoning, and embodied manipulation benchmarks.

Cubic Discrete Diffusion: Discrete Visual Generation on High-Dimensional Representation Tokens

CVPR

Yuqing Wang, Chuofan Ma, Zhijie Lin, Yao Teng, Lijun Yu, Shuai Wang, Jiaming Han, Jiashi Feng, Yi Jiang, Xihui Liu

AI Summary

Discrete visual generation is limited by low-dimensional latent tokens, sacrificing semantic richness crucial for unified multimodal architectures, while high-dimensional representations pose fundamental generation challenges. The authors propose Cubic Discrete Diffusion (CubiD), the first discrete generation model for high-dimensional representations, which employs fine-grained masking across all dimensions and positions for efficient prediction in fixed steps. CubiD achieves state-of-the-art discrete generation on ImageNet-256 and, importantly, the discretized high-dimensional tokens preserve their original capabilities for both understanding and generation tasks.

Not All Features Are Created Equal: A Mechanistic Study of Vision-Language-Action Models

ICLR

Bryce Grant, Xijia Zhao, Peng Wang

AI Summary

This study investigates how Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models translate multimodal inputs into actions using activation injection, sparse autoencoders, and linear probes across six models. It reveals the visual pathway predominantly drives action generation across architectures, utilizing spatially bound motor programs. Language sensitivity is task-dependent, becoming essential only when visual context is ambiguous and multiple goals exist. In multi-pathway architectures, expert pathways encode motor programs, while VLM pathways encode goal semantics within separable activation subspaces.

MonoArt: Progressive Structural Reasoning for Monocular Articulated 3D Reconstruction

arXiv

Haitian Li, Haozhe Xie, Junxiang Xu, Beichen Wen, Fangzhou Hong, Ziwei Liu

AI Summary

Reconstructing articulated 3D objects from a single image is challenging due to the entanglement of motion cues and object structure, leading to unstable direct articulation regression. MonoArt proposes a unified framework grounded in progressive structural reasoning, which transforms visual observations into canonical geometry, structured part representations, and motion-aware embeddings within a single architecture, avoiding direct articulation prediction. This method achieves state-of-the-art performance in both reconstruction accuracy and inference speed on PartNet-Mobility and generalizes to robotic manipulation and articulated scene reconstruction.

arXiv

AI Summary

CVPR

arXiv

AI Summary
