Generation Models Know Space: Unleashing Implicit 3D Priors for Scene Understanding

arXiv

Xianjin Wu, Dingkang Liang, Tianrui Feng, Kui Xia, Yumeng Zhang, Xiaofan Li, Xiao Tan, Xiang Bai

AI Summary

Multimodal LLMs struggle with fine-grained spatial reasoning and physical dynamics, with existing solutions limited by explicit 3D data scarcity. This paper proposes VEGA-3D, a plug-and-play framework that leverages the implicit 3D priors from pre-trained video diffusion models, repurposed as a Latent World Simulator. It extracts spatiotemporal features from the video model and integrates them with MLLM semantic representations via adaptive gated fusion, providing dense geometric cues without explicit 3D supervision. The method significantly outperforms state-of-the-art baselines across 3D scene understanding, spatial reasoning, and embodied manipulation benchmarks.

CVPR

AI Summary

Cubic Discrete Diffusion (CubiD) addresses the limitation of existing discrete generation models that rely on semantically poor, low-dimensional tokens. Not All Features Are Created Equal: A Mechanistic Study of Vision-Language-Action Models

ICLR

Bryce Grant, Xijia Zhao, Peng Wang

AI Summary

This research investigates how Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models translate multimodal inputs into robotic actions using activation injection, sparse autoencoders, and linear probes across six diverse architectures. The findings reveal that visual pathways dominate action generation through spatially bound motor programs, while language is utilized only when visual context is insufficient for goal disambiguation. In multi-pathway models, expert and VLM pathways occupy distinct activation subspaces, specializing in motor execution and goal semantics respectively.

arXiv

AI Summary
