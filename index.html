Act Wisely: Cultivating Meta-Cognitive Tool Use in Agentic Multimodal Models

arXiv

Shilin Yan, Jintao Tong, Hongwei Xue, Xiaojun Tang, Yangyang Wang, Kunyu Shi, Guannan Zhang, Ruixuan Li, Yixiong Zou

AI Summary

Agentic multimodal models exhibit a meta-cognitive deficit, leading to blind, unnecessary tool invocation even when internal knowledge suffices, causing latency and reasoning errors that current scalarized RL penalties fail to mitigate effectively. To address this, they propose HDPO, a framework that decouples tool efficiency from accuracy by employing two orthogonal optimization channels: one for task correctness and another for execution economy, enforced conditionally within accurate trajectories. Their resulting model, Metis, significantly reduces tool invocations by orders of magnitude while simultaneously elevating reasoning accuracy.

SIM1: Physics-Aligned Simulator as Zero-Shot Data Scaler in Deformable Worlds

arXiv

Yunsong Zhou, Hangxu Liu, Xuekun Jiang, Xing Shen, Yuanzhen Zhou, Hui Wang, Baole Fang, Yang Tian, Mulin Yu, Qiaojun Yu, Li Ma, Hengjie Li, Hanqing Wang, Jia Zeng, Jiangmiao Pang

AI Summary

Robotic manipulation of deformable objects is data-intensive, yet current sim-to-real pipelines are insufficient due to ungrounded rigid-body abstractions, causing mismatched geometry and fragile soft dynamics. SIM1 proposes a physics-aligned real-to-sim-to-real data engine that grounds simulation by digitizing scenes into metric-consistent twins, calibrating deformable dynamics, and expanding behaviors via diffusion-based trajectory generation. This method enables policies trained on purely synthetic data to achieve parity with real-data baselines at a 1:15 equivalence ratio, delivering 90% zero-shot success and 50% generalization gains in real-world deployment.

Seeing but Not Thinking: Routing Distraction in Multimodal Mixture-of-Experts

arXiv

Haolei Xu, Haiwen Hong, Hongxing Li, Rui Zhou, Yang Zhang, Longtao Huang, Hui Xue, Yongliang Shen, Weiming Lu, Yueting Zhuang

AI Summary

Multimodal Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) models suffer from "Seeing but Not Thinking," accurately perceiving visual content but failing in subsequent reasoning, unlike identical text-only problems. This issue stems from routing distraction, where image inputs induce routing divergence in middle layers, preventing adequate activation of task-relevant reasoning experts. To counteract this, the authors propose a routing-guided intervention method that enhances domain expert activation. This method achieves consistent improvements of up to 3.17% on complex visual reasoning tasks across multiple models and benchmarks, demonstrating transferability of identified cognitive functions.

OpenVLThinkerV2: A Generalist Multimodal Reasoning Model for Multi-domain Visual Tasks

arXiv

Wenbo Hu, Xin Chen, Yan Gao-Tian, Yihe Deng, Nanyun Peng, Kai-Wei Chang

AI Summary

Open-source multimodal generalist models face challenges with extreme reward variance across diverse visual tasks and difficulty balancing fine-grained perception with multi-step reasoning using GRPO-based RL. To address this, the authors propose Gaussian GRPO (G$^2$RPO), a novel RL objective that employs non-linear distributional matching to force advantage distributions to a standard normal, ensuring inter-task gradient equity and stability. Fail2Drive: Benchmarking Closed-Loop Driving Generalization

arXiv

Simon Gerstenecker, Andreas Geiger, Katrin Renz

AI Summary

Generalization under distribution shift is a core bottleneck for closed-loop autonomous driving, as current benchmarks often test memorization rather than true robustness. This paper introduces Fail2Drive, the first paired-route benchmark for closed-loop generalization in CARLA, featuring 200 routes and 17 new scenario classes that match shifted routes with in-distribution counterparts to isolate shift effects. Evaluating state-of-the-art models reveals consistent degradation, with an average 22.8\% success-rate drop. This analysis uncovered unexpected failure modes, including models ignoring clearly visible LiDAR objects and failing to learn fundamental concepts of free and occupied space.

arXiv

Yanwen Zou, Chenyang Shi, Wenye Yu, Han Xue, Jun Lv, Ye Pan, Chuan Wen, Cewu Lu

AI Summary

Current robot data collection methods are burdensome, unscalable, and fail to capture natural human perception-manipulation for zero-shot transfer. A-SLIP: Acoustic Sensing for Continuous In-hand Slip Estimation

arXiv

Uksang Yoo, Yuemin Mao, Jean Oh, Jeffrey Ichnowski

AI Summary

Reliable in-hand manipulation requires accurate real-time slip estimation, but existing tactile sensors face trade-offs in form factor, durability, and jointly sensing slip direction and magnitude. A-SLIP proposes a multi-channel acoustic sensing system with piezoelectric microphones behind a textured contact pad, using a lightweight convolutional network to process synchronized audio for joint prediction of slip presence, direction, and magnitude. The system achieved a mean absolute directional error of 14.1 degrees, outperformed baselines in detection accuracy by up to 12%, and significantly reduced directional and magnitude errors (up to 68%) compared to single-microphone configurations, demonstrating reliable, low-cost, real-time slip estimation.

arXiv

Joungbin An, Agrim Jain, Kristen Grauman

AI Summary

Video temporal grounding (VTG) suffers from dataset-specific models lacking transferability and MLLM-based solutions that are computationally expensive and struggle with long videos. UniversalVTG proposes a lightweight foundation model trained with large-scale cross-dataset pretraining, utilizing an offline Query Unifier to canonicalize diverse query formats and an efficient grounding head. Density-Driven Optimal Control: Convergence Guarantees for Stochastic LTI Multi-Agent Systems

arXiv

Kooktae Lee

AI Summary

The core problem addressed is decentralized non-uniform area coverage for multi-agent systems, often relying on computationally heavy or heuristic density-based methods. This paper proposes Stochastic Density-Driven Optimal Control (D$^2$OC), a rigorous Lagrangian framework that formulates a stochastic MPC-like problem minimizing the Wasserstein distance. The method ensures the time-averaged empirical distribution converges to a non-parametric target density under stochastic LTI dynamics. A key contribution is a formal convergence guarantee established via reachability analysis, providing bounded tracking error despite process and measurement noise, with numerical results verifying robust, decentralized coverage outperforming heuristics.

Faithful GRPO: Improving Visual Spatial Reasoning in Multimodal Language Models via Constrained Policy Optimization

arXiv

Sai Srinivas Kancheti, Aditya Kanade, Rohit Sinha, Vineeth N Balasubramanian, Tanuja Ganu

AI Summary

Multimodal reasoning models trained with reinforcement learning often achieve accuracy at the cost of reasoning quality, producing Chain-of-Thought (CoT) traces inconsistent with final answers and poorly grounded in visual evidence. To address this, Faithful GRPO (FGRPO) is proposed, which enforces logical consistency and visual grounding as constraints via Lagrangian dual ascent during policy optimization. FGRPO substantially improves reasoning quality, reducing CoT inconsistency from 24.5% to 1.7% and increasing visual grounding by +13%, while also enhancing final answer accuracy over standard GRPO.
