AI Summary

Existing pixel-level diffusion world models for autonomous driving suffer from high inference latency, preventing efficient high-frequency RL interaction. DreamerAD introduces a latent world model that achieves an 80x speedup by compressing diffusion sampling from 100 to 1 step via shortcut forcing, complemented by a latent-space autoregressive reward model and constrained exploration. arXiv

Jiaying Zhou, Zhihao Zhan, Ruifeng Zhai, Qinhan Lyu, Hao Liu, Keze Wang, Liang Lin, Guangrun Wang

AI Summary

Vision-Language-Action (VLA) policies struggle in cluttered scenes due to instance-level grounding failures, often grasping off-target or the wrong object. To address this, TAG (Target-Agnostic Guidance) is proposed as an inference-time mechanism inspired by classifier-free guidance. AI Summary

World-model-based autonomous driving planners are limited by inadequate representation compression, poor spatial understanding, and underutilized temporal dynamics, leading to sub-optimal planning with constrained data and compute. MARCH: Multi-Agent Reinforced Self-Check for LLM Hallucination

arXiv

Zhuo Li, Yupeng Zhang, Pengyu Cheng, Jiajun Song, Mengyu Zhou, Hao Li, Shujie Hu, Yu Qin, Erchao Zhao, Xiaoxi Jiang, Guanjun Jiang

AI Summary

LLMs face critical hallucination issues, especially in RAG, as existing detection methods using LLM-as-a-judge suffer from confirmation bias. MARCH proposes a multi-agent framework to address this, employing deliberate information asymmetry where a Solver generates a response, a Proposer decomposes it into claims, and a Checker validates these claims *in isolation* from the original output. This pipeline, trained with multi-agent reinforcement learning, substantially reduces hallucination rates. Notably, an 8B-parameter LLM equipped with MARCH achieves performance competitive with powerful closed-source models.

arXiv

Interlayer Coupling and Floquet-Driven Topological Phases in Bilayer Haldane Lattices

arXiv

Imtiaz Khan, Muzamil Shah, Reza Asgari, Gao Xianlong

AI Summary

This paper investigates Floquet-driven topological phase transitions in AB-stacked bilayer Haldane lattices, employing interlayer hybridization, intrinsic Haldane flux, circularly polarized light, and tunable hopping anisotropy. They demonstrate controlled transitions among Dirac, semi-Dirac, and higher-Chern insulating phases with Chern numbers up to $C=\pm2$, driven by competition between intrinsic and Floquet-induced masses. A key finding is that interlayer coupling induces helicity-dependent, valley-selective band inversions, stabilizing these higher-Chern phases. However, the topological phase space narrows and disappears at the semi-Dirac limit, making the system trivial beyond this point.

Cyclic adjoint modules and their embeddings in quantized enveloping algebras

arXiv

Arnab Bhattacharjee

AI Summary

This paper investigates cyclic adjoint modules within quantized enveloping algebras, analyzing the realization of irreducible modules via cyclic generators and describing fixed-type embeddings. This analysis reveals a natural map to isomorphism classes, whose fibers highlight the non-uniqueness of module realizations. The authors further introduce a partial order on cyclic adjoint modules, relating its minimal elements to irreducible submodules, and demonstrate that every cyclic adjoint module is generated by finitely many irreducible submodules.
