Universal Skeleton Understanding via Differentiable Rendering and MLLMs

arXiv

Ziyi Wang, Peiming Li, Xinshun Wang, Yang Tang, Kai-Kuang Ma, Mengyuan Liu

AI Summary

MLLMs cannot directly process structured non-visual data like human skeletons, as current methods are lossy or lack cross-format generalization. SkeletonLLM translates arbitrary skeleton sequences into the MLLM's native visual modality using DrAction, a differentiable, format-agnostic renderer that converts kinematics into compact image sequences. This end-to-end differentiable pipeline, enhanced by cooperative training, allows MLLM gradients to guide rendering. The method demonstrates strong generalization across diverse tasks including recognition, captioning, reasoning, and cross-format transfer, enabling MLLM application to non-native data.

Loc3R-VLM: Language-based Localization and 3D Reasoning with Vision-Language Models

arXiv

Kevin Qu, Haozhe Qi, Mihai Dusmanu, Mahdi Rad, Rui Wang, Marc Pollefeys

AI Summary

Multimodal Large Language Models struggle with spatial understanding and viewpoint-aware reasoning, often relying on augmented input rather than explicit 3D reasoning. Loc3R-VLM addresses this by equipping 2D Vision-Language Models with advanced 3D understanding from monocular video, using joint objectives for global layout reconstruction and explicit situation modeling, alongside camera pose priors. This direct spatial supervision enables state-of-the-art performance in language-based localization and outperforms existing methods on situated and general 3D question-answering benchmarks.

EchoGen: Cycle-Consistent Learning for Unified Layout-Image Generation and Understanding

AAAI

Kai Zou, Hongbo Liu, Dian Zheng, Jianxiong Gao, Zhiwei Zhao, Bin Liu

AI Summary

EchoGen addresses optimization challenges in jointly training layout-to-image generation and image grounding, which often hinder models from leveraging task duality. The framework employs a three-stage progressive strategy: parallel multi-task pre-training, dual joint optimization, and cycle reinforcement learning using consistency constraints as rewards. Experimental results demonstrate state-of-the-art performance on both generation and grounding benchmarks, confirming significant synergistic gains from the unified training approach.

GMT: Goal-Conditioned Multimodal Transformer for 6-DOF Object Trajectory Synthesis in 3D Scenes

arXiv

Huajian Zeng, Abhishek Saroha, Daniel Cremers, Xi Wang

AI Summary

GMT addresses the challenge of synthesizing precise 6-DOF object manipulation trajectories that account for full 3D scene geometry and physical feasibility. The proposed Goal-Conditioned Multimodal Transformer (GMT) generates continuous pose sequences by fusing 3D bounding boxes, point cloud context, semantic categories, and target end poses. Experiments demonstrate that GMT significantly outperforms state-of-the-art baselines in spatial accuracy and orientation control across both synthetic and real-world environments.

A Single-Fiber Optical Frequency Domain Reflectometry (OFDR)-Based Shape Sensing of Concentric Tube Steerable Drilling Robots

arXiv

Yash Kulkarni, Mobina Tavangarifard, Daniyal Maroufi, Mohsen Khadem, Justin E. Bird, Jeffrey H. Siewerdsen, Farshid Alambeigi

AI Summary

Traditional shape sensing for Concentric Tube Steerable Drilling Robots (CT-SDRs) is limited by the low spatial resolution and complex surface-integration requirements of conventional fiber Bragg grating sensors. This paper proposes a Shape Sensing Assembly (SSA) using a single Optical Frequency Domain Reflectometry (OFDR) fiber integrated with a NiTi wire, which is housed protectively within the robot's internal channel to enable continuous strain measurement. Experimental results from free-bending and phantom drilling tests demonstrate that this configuration provides accurate, robust, and reliable shape-sensing performance along complex trajectories.

AdaRadar: Rate Adaptive Spectral Compression for Radar-based Perception

CVPR

Jinho Park, Se Young Chun, Mingoo Seok

AI Summary

High-dimensional radar data for autonomous driving overwhelms low-bandwidth communication links, lacking an adaptive compression codec. AdaRadar proposes an online adaptive compression scheme that dynamically adjusts the compression ratio using zeroth-order gradient descent on detection confidence, combined with discrete cosine transform for selective coefficient pruning and scaled quantization. This method achieves over 100x feature size reduction with only a ~1%p performance drop, validated across multiple radar datasets.

Specification-Aware Distribution Shaping for Robotics Foundation Models

arXiv

Sadık Bera Yüksel, Derya Aksaray

AI Summary

Robotics foundation models lack formal guarantees for satisfying complex, time-dependent spatio-temporal specifications (e.g., safety, sequential objectives) during deployment. The authors propose a specification-aware action distribution optimization framework that enforces Signal Temporal Logic (STL) constraints during a pretrained model's execution without modifying its parameters. This method minimally modifies the action distribution at each step to ensure STL feasibility by reasoning over the future horizon using forward dynamics. The framework was validated in simulation using a state-of-the-art model across diverse environments and complex specifications.

VideoAtlas: Navigating Long-Form Video in Logarithmic Compute

arXiv

Mohamed Eltahir, Ali Habibullah, Yazan Alshoibi, Lama Ayash, Tanveer Hussain, Naeemullah Khan

AI Summary

Existing long-form video models rely on lossy text approximations and struggle to scale compute efficiently with increasing duration. VideoAtlas addresses this by representing video as a lossless, hierarchical grid, enabling "Video-RLM" to recursively navigate visual data via a parallel Master-Worker architecture. This approach achieves logarithmic compute scaling and maintains high accuracy on benchmarks up to 10 hours long, supported by a 30-60% multimodal cache hit rate and adaptive compute allocation.
