Gen-Searcher: Reinforcing Agentic Search for Image Generation

arXiv

Kaituo Feng, Manyuan Zhang, Shuang Chen, Yunlong Lin, Kaixuan Fan, Yilei Jiang, Hongyu Li, Dian Zheng, Chenyang Wang, Xiangyu Yue

AI Summary

Image generation models are fundamentally limited by frozen internal knowledge, causing failures in knowledge-intensive or dynamic scenarios. Gen-Searcher is introduced as the first search-augmented image generation agent, performing multi-hop reasoning and search to gather external textual knowledge and reference images for grounded generation. It is trained with SFT and agentic reinforcement learning using dual text and image-based rewards on custom datasets and a new benchmark. Gen-Searcher substantially improves baseline models, achieving gains of around 16 points on KnowGen and 15 points on WISE.

Adaptive Block-Scaled Data Types

arXiv

Jack Cook, Hyemin S. Lee, Kathryn Le, Junxian Guo, Giovanni Traverso, Anantha P. Chandrakasan, Song Han

AI Summary

NVFP4, a popular 4-bit quantization format, suffers from high quantization error on near-maximal values due to its fixed error distribution. To mitigate this, the paper proposes Adaptive Block-Scaled Data Types, notably IF4 for 4-bit quantization. IF4 dynamically selects between FP4 and INT4 representations for each 16-value block, leveraging the NVFP4 scale factor's unused sign bit to indicate the choice. This approach significantly outperforms existing 4-bit block-scaled formats, achieving lower loss in quantized training and higher accuracy in post-training quantization, with an efficient hardware implementation demonstrated.

On-the-fly Repulsion in the Contextual Space for Rich Diversity in Diffusion Transformers

arXiv

Omer Dahary, Benaya Koren, Daniel Garibi, Daniel Cohen-Or

AI Summary

Text-to-Image diffusion models suffer from a significant lack of variety and typicality bias, hindering creative applications, with existing diversity methods being either costly or disruptive. This paper proposes applying on-the-fly repulsion in the Contextual Space of Diffusion Transformers by intervening in multimodal attention channels between blocks. This method redirects the guidance trajectory after structural information emerges but before composition is fixed. The approach achieves significantly richer diversity without sacrificing visual fidelity or semantic adherence, proving efficient and effective even in "Turbo" and distilled models.

Topological Optical Chirality Dichroism

arXiv

Wojciech J. Jankowski, Giandomenico Palumbo, Robert-Jan Slager

AI Summary

The core problem is probing unobserved chiral electronic band topologies in three-dimensional materials. This paper proposes a method based on a universal topological dichroism arising from coupling optical chirality to higher tensor Berry curvatures and Dixmier-Douady invariants, with an experimental setup using superchiral light. Key results show chiral topological invariants yield integer-quantized dichroic excitation rate differences, establishing a new optical route for detecting these topologies.

arXiv

AI Summary

SOLE-R1: Video-Language Reasoning as the Sole Reward for On-Robot Reinforcement Learning

arXiv

Philip Schroeder, Thomas Weng, Karl Schmeckpeper, Eric Rosen, Stephen Hart, Ondrej Biza

AI Summary

Existing Vision-Language Models (VLMs) fail as robust reward signals for robot reinforcement learning due to partial observability and distribution shifts, leading to policies exploiting perceptual errors. This paper introduces SOLE-R1, a video-language reasoning model designed as the sole online reward, generating dense task progress from raw video and natural language goals via per-timestep spatiotemporal chain-of-thought reasoning. SOLE-R1 is trained with a large-scale synthesized video trajectory and reasoning data pipeline using a hybrid supervised fine-tuning and RL framework. It enables zero-shot online RL on 24 unseen manipulation tasks in simulation and real-robot settings, outperforming state-of-the-art VLMs (e.g., GPT-5, Gemini-3-Pro) and showing superior robustness to reward hacking.

Stepwise Credit Assignment for GRPO on Flow-Matching Models

CVPR

Yash Savani, Branislav Kveton, Yuchen Liu, Yilin Wang, Jing Shi, Subhojyoti Mukherjee, Nikos Vlassis, Krishna Kumar Singh

AI Summary

Existing Flow-GRPO uses uniform credit assignment in diffusion models, which fails to account for the temporal significance of early vs. late generation steps and can reward suboptimal intermediate actions. Stepwise-Flow-GRPO addresses this by assigning credit based on each step's reward improvement, leveraging Tweedie's formula for intermediate reward estimates and introducing gain-based advantages. It also incorporates a DDIM-inspired SDE to improve reward quality while preserving stochasticity for policy gradients. This method achieves superior sample efficiency and faster convergence.

Dynamic Dual-Granularity Skill Bank for Agentic RL

arXiv

Songjun Tu, Chengdong Xu, Qichao Zhang, Yaocheng Zhang, Xiangyuan Lan, Linjing Li, Dongbin Zhao

AI Summary

Agentic RL lacks fine-grained skill extraction and principled mechanisms for dynamically maintaining an evolving skill memory. D2Skill introduces a dynamic dual-granularity skill bank, organizing reusable experience into high-level task skills and fine-grained step skills, trained jointly with the policy using performance gaps to derive hindsight utility signals for continuous expansion and maintenance. DreamLite: A Lightweight On-Device Unified Model for Image Generation and Editing

arXiv

Kailai Feng, Yuxiang Wei, Bo Chen, Yang Pan, Hu Ye, Songwei Liu, Chenqian Yan, Yuan Gao

AI Summary

Large diffusion models are inefficient for on-device deployment, and existing on-device models primarily focus on text-to-image (T2I) generation, lacking unified support for image editing. DreamLite proposes a compact (0.39B) unified on-device diffusion model, built on a pruned mobile U-Net, that unifies conditioning for both T2I generation (target|blank) and editing (target|source) through in-context spatial concatenation in the latent space, trained via a task-progressive joint strategy. AI Summary

ACL

Yulie Arad, Stav Ashur, Marta Markowicz, James D. Motes, Marco Morales, Nancy M. Amato

AI Summary

FL-PBM: Pre-Training Backdoor Mitigation for Federated Learning

arXiv

Osama Wehbi, Sarhad Arisdakessian, Omar Abdel Wahab, Azzam Mourad, Hadi Otrok, Jamal Bentahar

AI Summary

Backdoor attacks pose a significant threat to AI model integrity in federated learning, requiring proactive mitigation, especially during the pre-training phase. The FL-PBM method proposes a client-side defense that employs benign trigger insertion, PCA for feature extraction, GMM clustering to identify malicious data, and targeted blurring to disrupt potential backdoor triggers before model training. Experimental results show FL-PBM reduces attack success rates by up to 95% compared to FedAvg and 30-80% against state-of-the-art defenses, while maintaining over 90% clean model accuracy.

Mitigating Backdoor Attacks in Federated Learning Using PPA and MiniMax Game Theory

arXiv

Osama Wehbi, Sarhad Arisdakessian, Omar Abdel Wahab, Anderson Avila, Azzam Mourad, Hadi Otrok

AI Summary

Federated Learning faces backdoor attacks from malicious clients who inject poisoned data, compromising the global model's integrity and leading to false predictions. To address this, the authors propose FedBBA, which combines a client reputation system, an incentive mechanism, and game-theoretical models with Projection Pursuit Analysis (PPA) to dynamically identify and minimize malicious client impact. FedBBA significantly reduced backdoor attack success rates to 1.1%-11% on GTSRB and BTSC datasets, outperforming state-of-the-art defenses (23%-76% attack success) while maintaining high normal task accuracy (95%-98%).

Neural Quantum States in Non-Stabilizer Regimes: Benchmarks with Atomic Nuclei

arXiv

James W. T. Keeble, Alessandro Lovato, Caroline E. P. Robin

AI Summary

The study addresses the open question of which properties determine the representational capabilities of Neural Quantum States (NQS) for complex, entangled quantum states. Using a second-quantized Restricted Boltzmann Machine (RBM) NQS, the ground states of medium-mass atomic nuclei, characterized by significant entanglement and non-stabilizerness, were represented. The key finding is that states exhibiting larger non-stabilizerness are systematically harder for RBMs to learn, leading to reduced accuracy. This suggests non-stabilizerness is a primary factor governing the compression and representational efficiency of RBMs in entangled regimes.
