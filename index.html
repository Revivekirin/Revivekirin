Act Wisely: Cultivating Meta-Cognitive Tool Use in Agentic Multimodal Models

arXiv

Shilin Yan, Jintao Tong, Hongwei Xue, Xiaojun Tang, Yangyang Wang, Kunyu Shi, Guannan Zhang, Ruixuan Li, Yixiong Zou

AI Summary

Agentic multimodal models exhibit a meta-cognitive deficit, leading to blind tool invocation and over-reliance on external utilities even when internal knowledge is sufficient, causing latency and reasoning errors, which existing scalarized reward RL methods fail to mitigate. To address this, the authors propose HDPO, a framework that reframes tool efficiency as a conditional objective, employing two orthogonal optimization channels: one for task accuracy and another for execution economy exclusively within accurate trajectories. This decoupled approach allows their resulting model, Metis, to reduce tool invocations by orders of magnitude while simultaneously elevating reasoning accuracy.

SIM1: Physics-Aligned Simulator as Zero-Shot Data Scaler in Deformable Worlds

arXiv

Yunsong Zhou, Hangxu Liu, Xuekun Jiang, Xing Shen, Yuanzhen Zhou, Hui Wang, Baole Fang, Yang Tian, Mulin Yu, Qiaojun Yu, Li Ma, Hengjie Li, Hanqing Wang, Jia Zeng, Jiangmiao Pang

AI Summary

Robotic manipulation with deformable objects is data-intensive, yet current sim-to-real pipelines fail due to rigid-body abstractions, leading to mismatched dynamics and geometry. SIM1 addresses this by introducing a physics-aligned real-to-sim-to-real data engine that grounds simulation through scene digitization, deformable dynamics calibration, and diffusion-based trajectory generation. Policies trained on SIM1's synthetic data achieve parity with real-data baselines at a 1:15 equivalence ratio, demonstrating 90% zero-shot success and 50% generalization gains in real-world deployment.

Seeing but Not Thinking: Routing Distraction in Multimodal Mixture-of-Experts

arXiv

Haolei Xu, Haiwen Hong, Hongxing Li, Rui Zhou, Yang Zhang, Longtao Huang, Hui Xue, Yongliang Shen, Weiming Lu, Yueting Zhuang

AI Summary

Multimodal Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) models suffer from "Seeing but Not Thinking," where they perceive images but fail visual reasoning tasks, attributed to routing distraction that prevents activation of relevant reasoning experts. The paper identifies layer-wise separation of visual and domain experts and routing divergence for image inputs, proposing a routing-guided intervention method to enhance domain expert activation. This intervention achieves consistent improvements of up to 3.17% on complex visual reasoning tasks across various models and benchmarks. The analysis further suggests this method identifies cognitive functions transferable across tasks.

AI Summary

arXiv

Wenbo Hu, Xin Chen, Yan Gao-Tian, Yihe Deng, Nanyun Peng, Kai-Wei Chang

AI Summary

Fail2Drive: Benchmarking Closed-Loop Driving Generalization

arXiv

Simon Gerstenecker, Andreas Geiger, Katrin Renz

AI Summary

Existing closed-loop autonomous driving benchmarks fail to truly measure generalization under distribution shifts, often reflecting memorization rather than robust behavior. To address this, Fail2Drive is introduced as the first paired-route benchmark in CARLA, featuring 200 routes across 17 new scenario classes that isolate the effect of various shifts. Evaluating state-of-the-art models on Fail2Drive revealed consistent performance degradation, with an average success-rate drop of 22.8%. Analysis further uncovered unexpected failure modes, including ignoring clearly visible LiDAR objects and failing to learn basic spatial concepts.

ActiveGlasses: Learning Manipulation with Active Vision from Ego-centric Human Demonstration

arXiv

Yanwen Zou, Chenyang Shi, Wenye Yu, Han Xue, Jun Lv, Ye Pan, Chuan Wen, Cewu Lu

AI Summary

The core problem is the burdensome and unnatural data collection for robot manipulation, hindering scalable capture of human perception-manipulation and zero-shot transfer. ActiveGlasses proposes a system where a smart glasses-mounted stereo camera captures ego-centric human demonstrations, then the *same* camera is re-mounted on a robot's perception arm to reproduce active vision. A-SLIP: Acoustic Sensing for Continuous In-hand Slip Estimation

arXiv

Uksang Yoo, Yuemin Mao, Jean Oh, Jeffrey Ichnowski

AI Summary

Reliable in-hand manipulation requires accurate, real-time slip estimation, a challenge for existing tactile sensors due to trade-offs in form factor, durability, and joint direction/magnitude sensing. A-SLIP introduces a multi-channel acoustic sensing system within a gripper, utilizing piezoelectric microphones behind a textured pad to capture vibrations processed by a lightweight convolutional network for slip presence, direction, and magnitude. The four-microphone configuration achieved a mean absolute directional error of 14.1 degrees, outperforming baselines and significantly reducing errors (64% directional, 68% magnitude) compared to single-microphone setups. This enables reliable, low-cost, real-time in-hand slip estimation.

Density-Driven Optimal Control: Convergence Guarantees for Stochastic LTI Multi-Agent Systems

arXiv

Kooktae Lee

AI Summary

This paper addresses the decentralized non-uniform area coverage problem for multi-agent systems, where existing density-based methods are computationally heavy or heuristic. It proposes Stochastic Density-Driven Optimal Control (D$^2$OC), a rigorous Lagrangian framework that formulates a stochastic MPC-like problem minimizing the Wasserstein distance to match a target density under stochastic LTI dynamics. The method provides formal convergence guarantees via reachability analysis, ensuring the time-averaged empirical distribution converges to the target with a bounded tracking error despite noise. Numerical results confirm robust, decentralized coverage and superior optimality and consistency compared to heuristic methods.

Faithful GRPO: Improving Visual Spatial Reasoning in Multimodal Language Models via Constrained Policy Optimization

arXiv

Sai Srinivas Kancheti, Aditya Kanade, Rohit Sinha, Vineeth N Balasubramanian, Tanuja Ganu

AI Summary

Reinforcement learning-trained multimodal reasoning models often produce Chain-of-Thought (CoT) traces that are inconsistent with their final answers and poorly grounded in visual evidence, despite accuracy gains. To address this, the paper proposes Faithful GRPO (FGRPO), a variant of Group Relative Policy Optimization that enforces logical consistency and visual grounding as constraints via Lagrangian dual ascent. FGRPO substantially improves reasoning quality, reducing CoT inconsistency from 24.5% to 1.7% and boosting visual grounding by 13%, while also enhancing final answer accuracy.
