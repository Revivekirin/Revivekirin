Act Wisely: Cultivating Meta-Cognitive Tool Use in Agentic Multimodal Models

arXiv

Shilin Yan, Jintao Tong, Hongwei Xue, Xiaojun Tang, Yangyang Wang, Kunyu Shi, Guannan Zhang, Ruixuan Li, Yixiong Zou

AI Summary

Agentic multimodal models exhibit a meta-cognitive deficit, struggling to discern when to use internal knowledge versus external tools, leading to blind, unnecessary tool invocation that causes latency and noise, a problem traditional scalarized RL penalties fail to resolve. We propose HDPO (Hierarchical Decoupled Policy Optimization), a framework that reframes tool efficiency as a conditional objective, using two orthogonal optimization channels for accuracy and *conditional* efficiency. This allows the model to first master task resolution before refining self-reliance. Our resulting model, Metis, dramatically reduces tool invocations by orders of magnitude while simultaneously elevating reasoning accuracy.

SIM1: Physics-Aligned Simulator as Zero-Shot Data Scaler in Deformable Worlds

arXiv

Yunsong Zhou, Hangxu Liu, Xuekun Jiang, Xing Shen, Yuanzhen Zhou, Hui Wang, Baole Fang, Yang Tian, Mulin Yu, Qiaojun Yu, Li Ma, Hengjie Li, Hanqing Wang, Jia Zeng, Jiangmiao Pang

AI Summary

Robotic manipulation of deformable objects is data-intensive, and existing rigid-body-centric sim-to-real pipelines fail to accurately model their complex dynamics, hindering data scaling. The proposed method, SIM1, is a physics-aligned real-to-sim-to-real data engine that grounds simulation by digitizing real scenes into metric-consistent twins, calibrating deformable dynamics, and expanding behaviors via diffusion-based trajectory generation. Policies trained on SIM1's synthetic data achieve parity with real-data baselines at a 1:15 ratio, delivering 90% zero-shot success and 50% generalization gains in real-world deployment.

Seeing but Not Thinking: Routing Distraction in Multimodal Mixture-of-Experts

arXiv

Haolei Xu, Haiwen Hong, Hongxing Li, Rui Zhou, Yang Zhang, Longtao Huang, Hui Xue, Yongliang Shen, Weiming Lu, Yueting Zhuang

AI Summary

Multimodal Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) models suffer from "Seeing but Not Thinking," where they perceive visual content but fail in subsequent reasoning tasks solvable as text, due to routing mechanisms inadequately activating task-relevant reasoning experts for image inputs. Through systematic analysis revealing layer-wise expert separation and routing divergence, the authors propose a routing-guided intervention method to enhance domain expert activation. This intervention consistently improved performance by up to 3.17% on complex visual reasoning tasks across various MoE models and benchmarks, demonstrating that identified domain experts correspond to transferable cognitive functions.

arXiv

Ziwei Zhou, Zeyuan Lai, Rui Wang, Yifan Yang, Zhen Xing, Yuqing Yang, Qi Dai, Lili Qiu, Chong Luo

AI Summary

The core problem is the fragmented evaluation of Text-to-Audio-Video (T2AV) generation, which currently lacks fine-grained assessment of joint audio-visual correctness for realistic prompts. The proposed method is AVGen-Bench, a task-driven benchmark with 11 real-world categories, coupled with a multi-granular evaluation framework that combines lightweight specialist models and Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs). arXiv

Wenbo Hu, Xin Chen, Yan Gao-Tian, Yihe Deng, Nanyun Peng, Kai-Wei Chang

AI Summary

Open-source multimodal generalist models leveraging GRPO face challenges due to extreme reward variance across diverse visual tasks and difficulty balancing fine-grained perception with multi-step reasoning. To address this, the authors introduce Gaussian GRPO (G$^2$RPO), a novel RL objective that forces advantage distributions to a standard normal, ensuring inter-task gradient equity and robustness. Fail2Drive: Benchmarking Closed-Loop Driving Generalization

arXiv

Simon Gerstenecker, Andreas Geiger, Katrin Renz

AI Summary

Generalization under distribution shift remains a core bottleneck for closed-loop autonomous driving, as existing benchmarks often measure memorization rather than true generalization. The authors introduce Fail2Drive, a novel paired-route benchmark in CARLA featuring 200 routes and 17 new scenario classes covering diverse appearance, layout, and behavioral shifts, designed to isolate and quantify generalization failures. A-SLIP: Acoustic Sensing for Continuous In-hand Slip Estimation

arXiv

Uksang Yoo, Yuemin Mao, Jean Oh, Jeffrey Ichnowski

AI Summary

Reliable in-hand manipulation necessitates accurate real-time slip estimation, a challenge for existing tactile sensors due to trade-offs in form factor, durability, and comprehensive slip direction/magnitude sensing. A-SLIP proposes a multi-channel acoustic sensing system integrated into a gripper, using piezoelectric microphones behind a textured silicone pad to capture contact-induced vibrations. A lightweight convolutional network processes synchronized multi-channel audio to jointly predict slip presence, direction, and magnitude. This system achieved a mean absolute directional error of 14.1 degrees, outperformed baselines, and significantly reduced directional (64%) and magnitude (68%) errors compared to single-microphone configurations, enabling reliable closed-loop reactive control.

arXiv

AI Summary

Large Language Models (LLMs) face a core problem of conflicts of interest when integrated with advertising, where user welfare (e.g., optimal product recommendation) can diverge from company revenue incentives. Visually-grounded Humanoid Agents

arXiv

Hang Ye, Xiaoxuan Ma, Fan Lu, Wayne Wu, Kwan-Yee Lin, Yizhou Wang

AI Summary

Existing digital human systems are passively animated, failing to exhibit active, goal-directed behavior using only visual observations in novel environments. This paper proposes Visually-grounded Humanoid Agents, a two-layer paradigm where a World Layer reconstructs semantically rich 3D Gaussian scenes and animatable avatars from real-world videos. An Agent Layer then equips these avatars with first-person RGB-D perception for embodied planning with spatial awareness and iterative reasoning, driving full-body actions within the scene. Experiments demonstrate robust autonomous behavior, achieving higher task success rates and fewer collisions compared to ablations and state-of-the-art planning methods.

Density-Driven Optimal Control: Convergence Guarantees for Stochastic LTI Multi-Agent Systems

arXiv

Kooktae Lee

AI Summary

The paper addresses the decentralized non-uniform area coverage problem for multi-agent systems, which existing density-based methods often tackle with computationally heavy PDE solvers or heuristics. It proposes Stochastic Density-Driven Optimal Control (D$^2$OC), a rigorous Lagrangian framework formulating a stochastic MPC-like problem that minimizes the Wasserstein distance to bridge individual agent dynamics with collective distribution matching. This approach guarantees convergence of the time-averaged empirical distribution to a non-parametric target density with a formally bounded tracking error, robustly outperforming previous heuristic methods in optimality and consistency.

arXiv

AI Summary

Faithful GRPO: Improving Visual Spatial Reasoning in Multimodal Language Models via Constrained Policy Optimization

arXiv

Sai Srinivas Kancheti, Aditya Kanade, Rohit Sinha, Vineeth N Balasubramanian, Tanuja Ganu

AI Summary

Multimodal Language Models (MRMs) trained with reinforcement learning often achieve accuracy gains at the cost of Chain-of-Thought (CoT) inconsistency with final answers and poor visual grounding. To address this, the paper proposes Faithful GRPO (FGRPO), a variant of Group Relative Policy Optimization that enforces batch-level logical consistency and visual grounding as constraints via Lagrangian dual ascent. FGRPO substantially reduces CoT inconsistency rates (24.5% to 1.7%), improves visual grounding scores by +13%, and also enhances final answer accuracy over standard GRPO.
