VOSR: A Vision-Only Generative Model for Image Super-Resolution

CVPR

Rongyuan Wu, Lingchen Sun, Zhengqiang Zhang, Xiangtao Kong, Jixin Zhao, Shihao Wang, Lei Zhang

AI Summary

Recent generative image super-resolution (SR) methods rely on adapting large, costly text-to-image (T2I) models despite SR being a vision-only restoration task. VOSR proposes a Vision-Only generative framework using a pretrained vision encoder for LR visual guidance and a novel restoration-oriented guidance strategy instead of standard unconditional branches, trained purely on visual data and distilled for efficiency. VOSR achieves competitive or superior perceptual quality and efficiency with less than one-tenth the training cost of T2I-based methods. It produces more faithful structures with fewer hallucinations, demonstrating high-quality generative SR without multimodal pretraining.

The Compression Gap: Why Discrete Tokenization Limits Vision-Language-Action Model Scaling

arXiv

Takuya Shiba

AI Summary

Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models fail to scale as expected with vision encoder upgrades when actions are represented as discrete tokens. This is attributed to the "Compression Gap" principle, where scaling behavior is governed by the tightest information bottleneck in the pipeline. When actions are continuous, the vision encoder is the bottleneck, but with discrete actions through a fixed-capacity codebook, the codebook itself becomes the binding constraint. Experiments validate this: continuous action models significantly improve with encoder upgrades, while discrete models show attenuated gains, remain flat across encoder quality, and only partially recover sensitivity by increasing codebook capacity.

arXiv

AI Summary

This work investigates quantum contact processes (QCPs) on topologically non-trivial lattices, exploring their dynamics and control beyond classical counterparts. arXiv

Yingxu Wang, Kunyu Zhang, Jiaxin Huang, Mengzhu Wang, Mingyan Xiao, Siyang Gao, Nan Yin

AI Summary

Graph Domain Adaptation (GDA) struggles with structural discrepancies between source and target graphs, as existing methods overlook topological shifts critical for reliable GNN transfer. DSBD proposes Dual-Aligned Structural Basis Distillation, which constructs a differentiable structural basis from probabilistic prototype graphs, optimized for topology. This basis is learned under source supervision and explicitly aligned to the target domain through dual objectives: geometric consistency via topological moment matching and spectral consistency via Dirichlet energy calibration, enabling decoupled GNN inference. DSBD consistently outperforms state-of-the-art GDA methods on various graph and image benchmarks.

Understanding the complexity of frequency and phase angle fluctuations in power grids

arXiv

Alessandro Lonardi, Jacques M. Maritz, Leonardo Rydin Gorjão, Christian Beck

AI Summary

The core problem is the limited understanding of stochastic frequency and phase-angle fluctuations in power grids, crucial for modernization and stability amidst increasing renewable penetration and market influences. The authors collected a large-scale, high-resolution dataset from the UK and South Africa and developed an analytical superstatistical model incorporating market-driven power fluctuations as a slowly varying parameter and nonlinear frequency control. This model accurately reproduces multimodal frequency distributions, heavy-tailed fluctuations, and double-exponential autocorrelation decays, aligning with experimental data. Furthermore, they demonstrated that a low-dimensional effective grid model precisely fits spatial phase-angle fluctuations in complex grids like South Africa, clarifying how markets and control policies shape grid dynamics.

Minimal Information Control Invariance via Vector Quantization

arXiv

Ege Yuceel, Teodor Tchalakov, Sayan Mitra

AI Summary

Safety-critical autonomous systems require minimal control signal complexity to ensure forward invariance given tight computational and sensing constraints. This paper proposes a vector-quantized autoencoder that jointly learns a state-space partition and a finite control codebook.
