arXiv

AI Summary

Disentangled Point Diffusion for Precise Object Placement

arXiv

Lyuxing He, Eric Cai, Shobhit Aggarwal, Jianjun Wang, David Held

AI Summary

Robotic manipulation policies struggle with precision and generalization to novel object geometries in placement tasks. The proposed method, TAX-DPD, is a hierarchical, disentangled point diffusion framework that uses a Dense Gaussian Mixture Model for global placements and a novel disentangled point cloud diffusion module for local object configuration. CVPR

Junhao Chen, Kejun Gao, Yuehan Cui, Mingze Sun, Mingjin Chen, Shaohui Wang, Xiaoxiao Long, Fei Ma, Qi Tian, Ruqi Huang, Hao Zhao

AI Summary

Existing generative models cannot produce vector animations, which offer resolution-independence and editability, because they operate exclusively in raster space. This work presents LottieGPT, the first framework for autoregressively generating vector animations. It tokenizes the Lottie animation standard into compact, semantically aligned sequences using a tailored Lottie Tokenizer and leverages the new LottieAnimation-660K dataset to finetune Qwen-VL. arXiv

arXiv

Haojie Bai, Aimin Li, Ruoyu Yao, Xiongwei Zhao, Tingting Zhang, Xing Zhang, Lin Gao, and Jun Ma

AI Summary

Existing diffusion planners for cooperative multi-agent driving lack scene consistency, are poorly aligned with closed-loop objectives, and are difficult to stably fine-tune online. Multi-ORFT proposes a two-stage approach combining scene-conditioned diffusion pre-training, leveraging inter-agent attention and AdaLN-Zero for consistent joint trajectories, with stable online reinforcement post-training. This post-training utilizes a two-level MDP exposing reverse-kernel likelihoods and Variance-Gated Group-Relative Policy Optimization (VG-GRPO) for stabilization. On the WOMD benchmark, Multi-ORFT significantly reduced collision (2.04% to 1.89%) and off-road rates (1.68% to 1.36%) while increasing average speed (8.36 to 8.61 m/s) compared to its pre-trained version, also outperforming strong baselines.

arXiv
